To comprehensively evaluate and compare the performance of the aforementioned option pricing methods under the 3/2 volatility model, this study designed multiple sets of parameter scenarios encompassing both normal and extreme market environments, conducting exhaustive numerical experiments.
\section{Data Sources and Scenario Design}
The benchmark pricing data in this study primarily originates from two sources:
* Exact option price data were extracted from existing literature to serve as benchmarks, covering some of the extreme scenarios.
* For other extreme scenarios not covered in the literature, option prices generated by the Euler/Milstein scheme with an extremely fine step size were used as baseline references.

To systematically test the robustness of the pricing algorithms, this study established seven distinct parameter combinations (Case I through Case VII), representing various market scenarios:
* Case I: Follow Baldeaux (2012)'s approach for at-the-money (ATM) options.
* Case II, Case III: Refer to Kouarfate et al. (2021)'s setting for at-the-money options.
* Case IV: Near expiration and at-the-money options (Near expiration and atm).
* Case V: Only near expiration.
* Case VI: Only at-the-money options (atm only).
* Case VII: Follow the classic setting of Lewis (2000).
