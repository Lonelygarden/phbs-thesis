\chapter{Literature Review}

The trajectory of stochastic volatility modeling highlights a growing reliance on the $3/2$ framework, driven by its non-linear architecture and empirical resilience during severe market dislocations. This chapter critically examines the existing body of literature, tracing the structural extensions of the model before detailing the progression of exact simulation algorithms.

\section{Development of the 3/2 Model and its Extended Frameworks}

Recognizing the descriptive constraints of early volatility paradigms, theoretical advancements have largely focused on expanding the boundaries and analytical tractability of the 3/2 model. Rather than modeling instantaneous volatility directly, \citet{carrNewApproachOption2007} bypassed the specification of the volatility risk premium by targeting the variance swap rate. Concurrently, \citet{medvedevApproximationCalibrationShortTerm2007} utilized asymptotic expansions to tackle the pricing complexities inherent in non-affine local structures like the 3/2 model.

Introduction of Jumps and Unified Models: To achieve consistent modeling of equity and VIX derivatives, \citet{baldeauxConsistentModelingVIX2012} introduced the "3/2 plus jumps model". More representatively, \citet{grasselli42Stochastic2017} proposed the "4/2 stochastic volatility model", a versatile framework that subsumes both the Heston and the 3/2 models as special cases. By assuming the instantaneous variance is a linear combination of the 1/2 and 3/2 terms, this "4/2 model" effectively leverages the strengths of both predecessors.

Handling Non-affine Characteristics: Addressing the non-affine property of the 3/2 model, \citet{kouarfateExplicitSolutionSimulation2021} developed a novel simulation framework based on an explicit weak solution for option pricing. This framework ingeniously leverages the property that the variance process of the 3/2 model is the inverse of a CIR process.

\section{Evolution of Exact Simulation Methods}

Having established the foundational framework of the 3/2 model, achieving efficient pricing while avoiding time discretization errors became another major research priority. Academia has subsequently developed and continuously optimized a series of exact simulation methods.

Establishment of the Foundational Framework: \citet{broadieExactSimulationStochastic2006} suggested a method for the exact simulation of the stock price and variance under Heston’s stochastic volatility model and other affine jump diffusion processes, which is known as the exact MC scheme.

Initial Efficiency Improvements: Subsequently, \citet{glassermanGammaExpansionHeston2011} significantly improved simulation efficiency by avoiding the numerical inverse Laplace transform. Using results derived by \citet{pitmanDecompositionBesselBridges1982}, they decomposed the conditional integrated variance into an infinite series of gamma random variables.

Extension and Application to the 3/2 Model: Targeting the 3/2 model specifically, \citet{baldeauxEXACTSIMULATION322012} discussed the exact simulation of the stock price process underlying the model. Using a result derived by Craddock and Lennox via Lie Symmetry Analysis, he successfully adapted the Broadie-Kaya algorithm for affine processes to the 3/2 model.

Recent Breakthroughs and Innovative Algorithms: In recent years, algorithmic optimization in this field has achieved further breakthroughs. \citet{choiSimulationSchemesHeston2024} proposed a new exact simulation scheme without utilizing the modified Bessel function, based on the core observation that the conditional integrated variance can be simplified when conditioned by the Poisson variate used for simulating the terminal variance. Furthermore, \citet{brignoneExactSimulationStochastic2026} proposed a novel simulation framework leveraging the conditional COS method. This research addresses long-standing implementation challenges in exact simulation, particularly concerning error control and computational overhead observed since Broadie and Kaya (2006).

\section{Current State of Domestic Research}

While international discourse on the $3/2$ model is robust, domestic research in this specific domain remains in a nascent stage. The bulk of local financial engineering literature continues to heavily emphasize the Heston and SABR models. This disproportionate focus leaves a noticeable void regarding the $3/2$ volatility framework and its corresponding high-efficiency numerical algorithms, underscoring the practical necessity and academic value of the present thesis.

\section{Literature Review Summary and Research Positioning}

The reviewed literature confirms a crucial paradox: although the theoretical foundations of the $3/2$ model—ranging from the $4/2$ synthesis to gamma-expanded exact simulations—are well-established, the practical implementation of these pricing engines remains fragile. Standard methods consistently exhibit numerical breakdowns, uncontrollable error margins, or severe latency spikes when exposed to boundary conditions, such as extremely short maturities or at-the-money (ATM) strikes.Bridging this exact gap, this study pivots away from superficial algorithmic tweaks and fundamentally interrogates the underlying mathematical constraints. Specifically, our research:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Deconstructs core mathematical properties by rigorously analyzing the first-order partial and second-order derivatives of the modified Bessel function of the first kind, yielding explicit convergence thresholds.
    \item Formulates a universal, fast-pricing architecture capable of resolving the chronic valuation failures associated with ATM and near-maturity instruments across all parameter spectrums.
    \item Conducts a multidimensional empirical benchmark, definitively quantifying the accuracy-efficiency trade-offs among prevailing methods to guide optimal algorithm deployment in live quantitative applications.
\end{enumerate}
